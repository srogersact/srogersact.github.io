Chemistry Vocabulary 2

Directions:

See if you can think of the word or words that go in each blank. Then hover over the blank to see if you were right.


A substance that cannot be broken down further is called __________an element. Over a hundred __________elements make up all the solids, liquids, and gases around us.

The smallest bits of an element are __________atoms, each of which has the same number of __________protons. Two or more atoms joined together are called __________ a molecule. If this has two or more elements, it is called __________a compound, and its basic unit is __________a molecule.

The smaller parts of atoms are called __________subatomic particles. A subatomic particle having a positive charge is called __________a proton. A subatomic particle having a negative charge is called __________an electron. __________A neutron is a subatomic particle that is neutral. __________Protons and __________neutrons are located in the nucleus, while __________electrons are located in the orbitals.

An element’s __________atomic number tells you the number of protons in each atom, but you need to subtract this from the __________atomic mass to find the number of neutrons.

Forms of the same element always have equal numbers of __________protons, but their nuclei can have different numbers of __________neutrons. For example, carbon atoms always have six protons. Most carbon atoms also have six neutrons. However, there are also carbon atoms with seven or even eight neutrons. These varieties of carbon are called __________isotopes.

The __________chemical symbol is an abbreviation of an element’s name, such as C for carbon.
