Because of Winn-Dixie:
Conventions, Part 2

Some Reminders about Quotations

If the speech tag is before the quote...

Add a comma after the speech tag and before the quotation marks, like this:

Opal asked the preacherThis is the speech tag., "Can I keep Winn-Dixie?This is the quote."

Be sure to capitalize the first word in the quote.

If the speech tag is after the quote...

Ordinarily, add a comma after the quote and before the speech tag.

"I want to hear ten things about my mamaThis is the quote.," Opal told the preacherThis is the speech tag..

Don't add a comma in these cases:

if the quote is followed by a question mark...

"What's the next thing on the list?This is the quote." Opal asked the preacherThis is the speech tag..

...or if the quote is followed by an exclamation point.

"Grab that dog!This is the quote." he exclaimedThis is the speech tag..

If the speech tag is in the middle of the quote, things are more complicated...

See what you can learn by looking at these examples:

"He is a special dogThis is a complete sentence, but there's a comma at the end.," I told her.The speech tag has a period at the end."He catches mice in church.This is another complete sentence, starting with a capital."

"My dog, Winn-DixieThis is NOT a complete sentence. There's a comma at the end.," began Opal,The speech tag has a COMMA at the end to show the sentence is not complete yet."is the best mouse-catcher in town.This is the rest of the sentence. It does NOT start with a capital."
