A Reminder about Apostrophes

A contraction is a word with letters that are left out.
An apostrophe takes the place of the letters that are left out.

Add an apostrophe where letters are left out, like this:

What's The apostrophe takes the place of i in what is. the next thing on the list?

He's The apostrophe takes the place of i in he is. a special dog.

Opal asked the preacher, "Can't The apostrophe takes the place of n and o in cannot. I keep Winn-Dixie?"

Direct Quotes and Indirect Quotes

A direct quote uses quotation marks to give the exact words that the person spoke.
An indirect quote does not use quotation marks. It is often introduced by the word that.
