As you probably remember, the smallest form of matter having the properties of an element is __________an atom, and it is made of protons, neutrons, and electrons. A group of __________atoms bonded together is __________a molecule, which is the basic unit of a compound.

These tiny particles are constantly __________vibrating, which means moving rapidly and continuously to and fro. Since they are moving, atoms and molecules have __________kinetic energy, or energy from being in motion. We use a more specific term, __________thermal __________energy, when we are referring to the kinetic energy of the tiny particles like atoms and molecules that make up matter. You measure __________thermal energy whenever you use a thermometer, because the __________temperature of a substance is a measure of the average kinetic energy of its particles.

Particles that vibrate faster take up the more __________volume, which is what we call the amount of space objects occupy. __________A gas is the familiar form of matter that takes up the most space. __________A gas does not have a definite shape or volume. Its atoms or molecules have so much thermal energy that they do not stick together. Instead, they spread apart. When gas particles __________collide (strike or hit each other with force), they ricochet off instead of sticking together.

If a gas loses its __________thermal energy, the gas particles slow down and do not __________collide with so much force. They will start to stick together, and the gas will become a __________liquid. Like a __________gas, this state of matter does not have a definite shape and will flow into the shape of any container you put it into. However, unlike a __________gas, a __________liquid takes up a definite amount of space; in other words, it has a definite __________volume and will not expand to take up all the space you give it, like a __________gas.

We have thought about two common forms in which matter can exist—gases and liquids. That leaves just one more familiar __________state of __________matter to discuss. This form of matter has a definite volume, so it is more like a __________liquid than it is like a __________gas. However, it also has a definite shape so it does not flow like a liquid or a gas. (In other words, it is not a fluid.) Can you guess what state of matter has a definite shape as well as a definite __________volume? It’s a __________solid.

Changing from one form of matter to another—for example, from gas to liquid—is called a __________phase __________change. There are two sets of phase changes you are familiar with. One set is evaporation (going from __________liquid to __________gas) and its opposite, condensation (going from __________gas to __________liquid). The other set is melting (going from __________solid to __________liquid) and its opposite, freezing (going from __________liquid to __________solid ). Knowing the temperature ranges at which a substance is found as a solid, liquid, or gas helps us to identify the substance.
