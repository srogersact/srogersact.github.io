Using Commas

In this lesson you will learn how to use commas...
  • To correctly punctuate a compound sentence.
  • To correctly punctuate dialogue.

 Goal 1    Using commas in compound sentences.

A compound sentence really just two or more sentences joined together.

Here are two sentences:

The first story is about a gnat and a lion. The second story is about a gnat and a bull.

Look carefully to see how we join those two sentences using a comma and a coordinating conjunction:

The first story is about a gnat and a lion, and the second story is about a gnat and a bull.

 Goal 2   Using commas in quotations.


Commas are also used in quotations.
If a speaker tag comes before a quotation, a comma follows the speaker tag.
Look at where the comma is in this example:

The lion said, "Leave me alone, you pesky gnat!"


Now try that yourself!

Quotation Problem 1


If a speaker tag comes after a quotation, a comma follows the quotation. The comma goes before the final quotation marks.
Look at where the comma is in this example:

"You are such a braggart," the lion muttered.

Quotation Problem 2


If a speaker tag comes after a quotation that ends with a question mark or exclamation point, do not add a comma before the final quotation marks.
Look at this example:

"Why are you pestering me?" the lion whimpered.

And this example:

"Leave me alone!" the lion bellowed.
