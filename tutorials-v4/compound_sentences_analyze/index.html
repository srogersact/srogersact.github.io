Analyzing Compound Sentences

In this set of exercises you will analyze compound sentences. Analyze means "split."

For each sentence, you will do the following:
  • Get rid of the conjunction and, but, or or that joins the sentences.
  • You will usually make the comma into a period.
  • If the original sentence ended with a question mark, you will use question marks at the ends of the new sentences.
  • You will capitalize the first word of each sentence.
  • Sometimes you will replace but with However,— be sure to include that comma after "However"!
  • Sometimes you will replace or with Otherwise,— be sure to include that comma after "Otherwise"!
Hint: It may be helpful to review "Joining Sentences to Make Compound Sentences."
