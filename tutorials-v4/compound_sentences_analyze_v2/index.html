Analyzing Sentences

In this set of exercises you will analyze a few kinds of sentences. Analyze means "split."

For compound sentences, you will do the following:
  • Get rid of the conjunction and, but, or or that joins the sentences.
  • You will make the comma into a period.
  • You will capitalize the first word of each sentence.
  • You will replace but with However,— be sure to include that comma after "However."
  • Sometimes when you have or as the conjunction, you will add also after might and before the main verb.
Hint: It may be helpful to review "Analyzing Compound Sentences."

It is really important to know what to do with an appositive. Look at the following example:

When we learn about ancient technology, we study materials, the different kinds of stuff that are used to make things.

The appositive in this sentence is "the different kinds of stuff that are used to make things".
Now look at how to make that sentence into two sentences:

When we learn about ancient technology, we study materials. Materials are the different kinds of stuff that are used to make things.

Problem 6


Sometimes an appositive is in the middle of the sentence. Look at the following example:

Materials, the different kinds of stuff that are used to make things, are an important subject.

The appositive in this sentence is still "the different kinds of stuff that are used to make things."
Now look carefully at how to make that sentence into two sentences:

Materials are an important subject. Materials are the different kinds of stuff that are used to make things.
