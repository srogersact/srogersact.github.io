Subject predicate When? Where? Why? How?

This sentence demonstrates how conjunctions such as and, but, and or are marked.

Look at a few ways this sentence can be rearranged:

Opal and the preacher moved to Naomi, Florida, that summer.

Did you notice that the sentence has two subjects? It's called a "compound subject" sentence.
Here's another arrangement of the sentence:

The preacher and Opal moved to Naomi, Florida, that summer.

Did you see that the only difference was the order of the two subjects?

Here is another arrangement of the sentence:

That summer the preacher and Opal moved to Naomi, Florida.

Here are two similar sentences with some new information:

That summer they moved to Naomi, Florida, so he could be the new preacher at the Open Arms Baptist Church.

They moved that summer to Naomi, Florida, so he could be the new preacher at the Open Arms Baptist Church.

Here are two more arrangements:

So he could be the new preacher at the Open Arms Baptist Church, they moved that summer to Naomi, Florida.

So he could be the new preacher at the Open Arms Baptist Church, they moved to Naomi, Florida, that summer.
