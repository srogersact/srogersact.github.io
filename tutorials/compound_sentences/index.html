Compound Sentences

Learning about compound sentences is an easy place to begin analyzing and composing sentences.

In this work...
  • When you analyze a sentence, you will break it into simple sentences.
  • When you compose a sentence, you will join simple sentences together to make a more complicated sentence.

Analyzing a Compound Sentence

Here is a compound sentence:

Egypt is a country in Africa, and Greece is a country in Europe.

This sentence can easily be analyzed as two sentences.

You simply change the comma into a period and remove the conjunction:"And" is a conjunction--and so are "but" and "or."

Egypt is a country in Africa. Greece is a country in Europe.

That is super easy, don't you think?

Here is another very simple example:

Upper Egypt is in the south, and"And" is a conjunction. Lower Egypt is in the north.


Again, you just change the comma into a period and remove the conjunction."And" is a conjunction--and so are "but" and "or."

Upper Egypt is in the south. Lower Egypt is in the north.

We changed the comma to a period.

We took out the "and".

Composing a Compound Sentence

Here are two sentences:

Memphis was an important city in Egypt. Athens was an important city in Greece.

These sentences can easily be composed as one compound sentence.

You simply change the period into a comma and add a conjunction:"And" is a conjunction.

Memphis was an important city in Egypt, and Athens was an important city in Greece.

That is super easy, too, don't you think?

Here is another example:

The Ionian Sea is to the west of mainland Greece. The Aegean Sea is to the east of mainland Greece.

These are longer sentences. Do they make you nervous?

Write down the sentences.

Now change the period into a comma and add a conjunction:"And" is a conjunction.

The Ionian Sea is to the west of mainland Greece, and the Aegean Sea is to the east of mainland Greece.

We changed the period to a comma.

We added "and".

We un-capitalized the first letter of the second clause.A "clause" is about the same thing as a sentence, but it can be part of a sentence.

Ready to try that yourself?
